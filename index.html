The Beginning

Three years at College of Engineering Poonjar — Computer Engineering. Honestly? Felt less like college and more like a "Dark" (for those who've watched it) web series that I binge-lived.

👬

Friends? Absolute legends.

The kind who'd turn a normal day into a full Netflix series.

👨‍🏫

Teachers? One-of-a-kind.

The ones who knew absolutely everything, still tolerated our never-ending questions like it was part of their "teaching package" — half the time I couldn't even tell if they were teaching us or just hanging out with us.

📚

Academics?

Ah yes, those beautiful all-nighters where we 'studied' but mostly just discovered new ways to stay awake. Assignments magically completed the night before (sometimes the morning of).

💡

Our engineering project?

A miracle disguised as hard work — hours of 'brainstorming', arguments, reworks, and being tossed around like clothes in a dryer, that somehow transformed into a perfect presentation like nothing ever went wrong.

📝

Exams?

Basically surprise parties thrown by the syllabus — "oh, you thought you knew me? Guess again." Still, managed to make grades happen… don't ask me how.

Of course, it wasn't all chaos — somewhere in between the laughter, bunks, and late-night dramas, I actually learned a lot about Computer Engineering. From coding marathons to debugging nightmares, labs to projects, I walked away with real skills that will stick with me long after the memories of sleepless nights fade.

🏸 Badminton Court Showdowns

One of the greatest reasons for our class bunks, with wars so serious you'd think Olympic gold was on the line.

🥟 Legendary Canteen Puffs

That deserved their own fan club.

But the real degree? Surviving the chaos: bunks, our two-wheelers 🛵 and those random small trips, canteen debates more heated than lab vivas, tea breaks ☕ longer than lectures, and the eternal "what's for lunch?" battle.
Three years felt like a lifetime — laughter, fights, dumb decisions, legendary stories.

Now it's over. No replay button. Never getting days like those again — and maybe that's why they'll always hit different.

Eventful? Understatement of the century.
